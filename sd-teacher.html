6 Critical Checkpoints
‚úã #1: Hook Section (After predictions)
Goal: Every student wrote SOMETHING in all boxes.
Say: "I see you wrote [read response]. Tell me more." / "Just write your best guess."
Issue: Blank or "I don't know" ‚Üí "Have YOU ever wanted something you couldn't afford? Write that."
‚úã #2: Part 1 (After definitions)
Goal: Not copying hints‚Äîusing their own language.
Say: "Read your definition. Now explain it like I'm 10 years old." / "Give an example NOT from notes."

‚úã #3: Part 2 (After lemonade scenario)
Goal: Understanding WHY producers make more at higher prices (profit).
Say: "Why make MORE at $5 vs $1?" / "When would you STOP making lemonade?"
Issue: "Producers make more because people want it" ‚Üí "That's demand. What does PRODUCER care about?"
‚úã #4: Part 3 (After concert tickets)
Goal: Recognizing their own consumer behavior (limited budget).
Say: "Show of hands‚Äîwho'd pay $500?" / "What would you do instead?"

‚úã #5: Part 4 (After shortage/surplus scenarios)
Goal: THIS IS THE HARDEST. Distinguish shortage vs surplus, predict price movement.
Say: "Surplus = TOO MUCH stuff, prices fall. Shortage = NOT ENOUGH, prices rise."
If 30%+ got it wrong: STOP. Re-teach with different example before continuing.
‚úã #6: Part 5 (Before reflection)

Common Misconceptions & Fixes
Student Says... Quick Fix
"Price goes up because of demand" "Need BOTH sides: demand high AND supply low. What about the supply?"
"Supply curve goes down" "Think like a business: price UP, I make MORE. Curve goes up too. Demand is opposite."
"Equilibrium means prices never change" "Equilibrium is TODAY's balance. If supply/demand changes, new equilibrium tomorrow."
Can't identify shortage from scenario "More people want it than can get it = shortage. Too much made = surplus."
Differentiation Quick Guide
FOR SPED / STRUGGLING:
  • Read scenarios aloud
  • Pre-fill 2-3 blanks as examples
  • Word bank for key terms
  • Complete scenario #1 together before independent work
  • Sentence frame: "Price will ___ because ___"
  • Pair with strong peer
FOR ADVANCED:
  • "What if price stayed at $80 for a month?"
  • "At what price does surplus disappear?"
  • Create scenarios for classmates
  • Find current shortage/surplus in news
  • Peer tutor role during practice
