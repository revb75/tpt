EDTC 1017  ·  Guiding Student Behavior  ·  Dallas College Spring 2026 Field Reference — What to Know & What to Avoid
1
Your Role &
Professionalism
⚠ Common Trouble
Doing the teacher's job
You support. The teacher leads. Never grade, design lessons, or discipline alone.
Keeping student secrets
Always tell the supervising teacher. You cannot promise confidentiality to a student.
Talking about students in public
The break room, hallway, and parking lot are not private. Only debrief with your teacher — in private.
Posting about students on social media
No names, no photos, no descriptions — even from your personal account, even off the clock.
✏ Key Pedagogy
Professional Communication Use your school email. Be clear and respectful. When unsure what to share — ask the teacher first.
Know Your Role Help students during transitions, small groups, and individual work. Reinforce the teacher's instructions — do not replace them.
2
Students, Families
& Community
⚠ Common Trouble
Judging a student's behavior without context
Poverty, trauma, and home stress affect how students act. Ask "what happened to this student?" not "what is wrong with them?"
Talking to parents about student progress
Redirect to the teacher. You can be warm and kind — but do not share academic or behavioral observations with families.
Assuming a student is "just being difficult"
At-risk students signal need through behavior. Tiredness, absences, and withdrawal are signs — not excuses.
✏ Key Pedagogy
Asset-Based Mindset See students' cultures and languages as strengths. Never assume a quiet or struggling student does not care.
Relationship First Learn names. Say hello. Notice when someone is absent. Small acts build the trust that makes learning possible.
Behavior = Communication Ask: "What does this student need right now?" Respond with empathy before correction.
3
Helping Students
Learn
⚠ Common Trouble
Doing the work for the student
Ask a question instead of giving the answer. "What do you know so far?" is better than telling them the answer.
The "velcro aide" problem
Sitting too close, doing too much = dependence. Fade your support as students gain confidence. The goal is independence.
Only asking yes/no questions
Open questions push thinking. "Why do you think that?" is better than "Is that right?"
✏ Key Pedagogy
Bloom's Taxonomy — Ask at the right level Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze → Evaluate → Create. Push students to higher levels when ready.
Scaffolding Break tasks into small steps. Let students try each step before moving to the next. Remove support gradually.
Small-Group Instruction Closer proximity = faster feedback. Students can ask questions without fear. Address confusion immediately.
4
Classroom Behavior
& Teamwork
⚠ Common Trouble
Creating your own consequences for IEP students
The BIP is a legal document. Follow it exactly. Never take away recess or add punishments not in the plan.
Undermining the teacher's protocol
Always use the teacher's system first. Share new ideas privately after — never substitute your method during class.
Physical intervention without authorization
Most districts require CPI training before touching a student to stop a conflict. Use your voice first. Get help fast.
Taking sides in a student-teacher conflict
Never speak against the teacher to a student. Say: "Let's go talk to the teacher together."
✏ Key Pedagogy
CHAMPS — Teach expectations before each activity C = Conversation level  ·  H = How to get help  ·  A = Activity  ·  M = Movement  ·  P = Participation  ·  S = Success
De-escalation Steps Calm voice. Give space. Offer a choice. Say: "I can see you are frustrated. Let's figure this out together." Never argue publicly.
Reinforce What You Want to See "Thank you for getting started right away." Specific praise shapes behavior better than generic praise.
5
Career &
Professional Growth
⚠ Common Trouble
Underselling transferable skills
Childcare, tutoring, volunteering, caregiving — all count. Name the skill and connect it to the classroom role.
Using AI to write final assignments
AI tools are OK for brainstorming only. All final writing must be your own. Submitting AI text = academic integrity violation.
Generic interview answers
Use the STAR method: Situation → Task → Action → Result. Be specific. A real story is always stronger than a general answer.
✏ Key Pedagogy
STAR Method for Interviews Situation — what was happening?  Task — what did you need to do?  Action — what did you do?  Result — what happened?
Your Resume Must Show Any experience with children  ·  Communication & patience  ·  This course + any certifications  ·  Bilingual ability (a real strength — list it)
Your Educational Philosophy Not about the "right" answer. It shows who you are, why you are here, and how you will support students. Be honest and specific.

📖 Key Words & Meanings

IEPA legal plan for a student with a disability. Follow it exactly — you cannot change it.
BIPBehavior Intervention Plan. The exact steps to follow when a student has a behavior problem.
PBISSchool-wide system for teaching good behavior before problems happen.
FERPAFederal law that protects student records. Never share student information outside the professional chain.
CHAMPSFramework for teaching behavior expectations: Conversation, Help, Activity, Movement, Participation, Success.
ScaffoldingBreaking a task into smaller steps and removing support as the student gains confidence.
Bloom's Taxonomy6 levels of thinking: Remember, Understand, Apply, Analyze, Evaluate, Create.
ACEsAdverse Childhood Experiences — hard life events that affect how a student learns and behaves.
At-RiskA student who may need extra support. Behavior is often a signal of need, not defiance.
De-escalationSteps to calm a situation before it becomes a crisis. Use voice, space, and choice — not force.
Culturally ResponsiveTeaching that respects and connects to each student's culture, language, and community.
STAR MethodInterview structure: Situation → Task → Action → Result. Always use a real, specific story.
